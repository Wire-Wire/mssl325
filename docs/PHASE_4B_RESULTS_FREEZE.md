1. **Confounder-tested evidence for Dn < 0.5.** Both P1 and P3 carry documented caveats (P1: near-bin density CV = 0.93; P3: EB partially spike-dependent). No independent candidate reaches this range.

2. **Physical identification.** No window in this branch is identified as containing a plasma depletion layer, a compressed sheath, or any other specific physical structure. The metrics are measurement-model outputs, not physical diagnoses.

3. **Generalization.** Results are specific to THD (and one THE pass), under compressed-sheath conditions (Dp > ~3 nPa), with encounter-averaged boundaries. They do not represent the full dayside magnetosheath population.

4. **Threshold or detection semantics.** No Dn or EB value separates categories. No detector, threshold candidate, or classification rule is defined or implied.

### Why Dn < 0.5 remains cautious-only

- P1 (Dn = 0.12): near-bin density CV = 0.93; the Dn is driven by a noisy median, not a sustained spatial structure demonstrably free of wave or mirror-mode contamination.
- P3 (Dn = 0.39): EB is partially spike-dependent (Δ = 0.50 after spike removal); mapping sensitivity (±0.23 for ±1 nPa Dp) is the largest in the bank.
- No independent candidate reproduces Dn < 0.5 under the frozen model.

### Why THE Sep 19 does not validate a stronger low-Dn branch

- Dn = 0.76: below clean-core floor (0.94) but above cautious range (< 0.5).
- Membership = 84% (comparable to P1, the lowest in the main bank).

- Ma = 21.1 (extreme; boundary-model accuracy less tested).
